Many African SMEs still run their business on scattered spreadsheets and tools that don't talk to each other. It works… until growth exposes the cracks: duplicated data, stock errors, cash flow that's hard to track.

## The problem with generic solutions

International ERPs are powerful, but they're built for contexts that aren't always ours:

- payment flows that ignore **mobile money**;
- assumptions of permanent connectivity;
- license costs that are prohibitive for a growing SME.

The result: you adapt your business to the tool, instead of adapting the tool to your business.

## What a custom ERP brings

A system built around **your** real processes lets you:

1. **Centralize** sales, inventory, finance and HR into a single source of truth.
2. **Automate** repetitive tasks (invoicing, reminders, reports).
3. **Decide faster** thanks to dashboards tailored to your metrics.

> A good ERP doesn't just store data: it reveals what's really happening in your business.

## Where to start

There's no need to rebuild everything at once. We recommend a **modular** approach: start with the module that creates the most value (often inventory or invoicing), then expand gradually.
