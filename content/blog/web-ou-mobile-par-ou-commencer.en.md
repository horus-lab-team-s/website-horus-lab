It's one of the first questions we get asked: "Should we start with a website or a mobile app?" The right answer depends on your users and your goals.

## Start with your users

- **Are your customers mostly on mobile, with unstable connectivity?** A mobile app (or a PWA) makes sense.
- **Are you aiming for discovery and search visibility?** The web remains essential.
- **Tight budget and need to validate fast?** A **PWA** offers the best of both worlds.

## The case for a PWA

A *Progressive Web App* is a website that installs and behaves like an app: home-screen icon, offline mode, notifications. It's often the **ideal starting point** in our contexts:

1. a single codebase to maintain;
2. no app-store installation friction;
3. controlled costs.

> Native (iOS/Android) stays relevant when you need heavy performance or advanced hardware features.

## Our recommendation

First validate your idea with a simple version that's quick to bring to market, then invest in native once traction is proven. That's the **agile** approach we favor: deliver value early, then iterate.
